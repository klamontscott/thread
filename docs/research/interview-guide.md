# Thread — Interview Guide
15 minutes remote (Zoom/Google Meet). Record with permission.

---

## Before you start
- Have Thread open in a browser tab (thread-zeta-one.vercel.app) — don't share it yet
- Have a sample transcript ready to paste
- Start recording (with permission)

---

## Part 1: Current Process (5 min)

**"Tell me about the last time you synthesized an interview. Walk me through what you did."**

Listen for: tools used, time spent, what felt hard, workarounds

Probes (only if they don't come up naturally):
- "How did you decide what the key themes were?"
- "How confident were you that you captured everything important?"
- "What did you do with the output — who saw it?"
- "Was there anything you felt like you might have missed?"

---

## Part 2: Pain Points (3 min)

**"What's the most frustrating part of going from a raw interview to findings you can share?"**

Listen for: time, confidence, bias, formatting, stakeholder trust

Probes:
- "If you could change one thing about that process, what would it be?"
- "Have you ever shared findings and later realized you missed something important?"

---

## Part 3: Live Task with Thread (5 min)

**"I've been working on a tool that uses AI to help with this. I'd love to get your honest reaction. I'm going to share my screen and paste in a transcript — just think aloud as you look at the output."**

1. Share screen, open Thread
2. Paste sample transcript, run analysis
3. Let them react naturally — don't explain the UI

Listen for: first impressions, what they look at first, trust signals, confusion, skepticism

Probes (after they've had 30-60 seconds to look):
- "What's your first reaction?"
- "Do you trust these themes? Why or why not?"
- "What about the confidence badges — helpful or noise?"
- "Would you share this output with a PM or stakeholder as-is?"
- "What's missing?"

---

## Part 4: Close (2 min)

**"If this existed as a real tool, would you use it? What would need to change?"**

Final question: **"Is there anything I didn't ask about that you think I should know?"**

---

## After each interview
Capture immediately (within 5 min):
1. Top 3 quotes (exact words)
2. Biggest surprise
3. What they trusted / didn't trust about Thread's output
4. Would they use it? Why or why not?

---

## What you're looking for across all 5 interviews

| Signal | Case study use |
|--------|---------------|
| Time spent on manual synthesis | Problem validation — real numbers |
| Confidence gaps ("I worry I miss things") | Problem validation — emotional hook |
| First reaction to Thread output | Solution validation — does it land? |
| Trust in confidence badges / risk flags | Design decision validation |
| "Would you share this with stakeholders?" | Trust metric — the key measure |
| What's missing / what they'd change | Honest gaps for reflections section |
